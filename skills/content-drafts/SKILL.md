# Content Drafts Sub-Skill

**Role:** Create high-quality marketing content — blog posts, email campaigns, ad copy, landing pages, and more. Every piece follows brand voice, targets a specific audience, and serves a measurable business goal.

**Parent:** `marketing-lead` (orchestrator routes here for content creation tasks)

---

## Trigger

Activated by the marketing-lead orchestrator when:
- The customer requests any written content (blog, email, ad, landing page)
- A content calendar item is due for drafting
- An A/B test requires content variations
- Existing content needs updating or repurposing

## Content Types and Frameworks

### Blog Posts / Articles

**Structure:**
```
HEADLINE (6-12 words, specific, benefit-oriented)
META DESCRIPTION (150-160 characters, includes primary keyword, entices click)

HOOK (first paragraph — why should the reader care?)
  - Open with a problem, stat, question, or story
  - Make it personal: "you" language, not "we" language
  - Promise what the reader will get from reading

BODY (organized by subheadings)
  H2: [First main point]
    - Supporting evidence, examples, data
    - 2-4 paragraphs per section
  H2: [Second main point]
    - ...
  H2: [Third main point]
    - ...

CONCLUSION
  - Summarize key takeaways (3 bullet points)
  - Include CTA (one specific action)
  - No "in conclusion" — just conclude

CTA
  - What should the reader do next?
  - One action. Clear. Easy.
```

**SEO Guidelines:**
- Include primary keyword in: headline, first paragraph, one H2, meta description
- Include secondary keywords naturally (do NOT force them)
- Internal links to related content (if available)
- Target word count: 800-1500 words (long enough for SEO, short enough for attention spans)
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Include at least one list or table per post (aids readability and featured snippets)

### Email Campaigns

**Structure:**
```
SUBJECT LINE (30-50 characters ideal, 9 words max)
  - Specific and honest (no clickbait)
  - Create curiosity OR communicate clear value
  - Test: Would you open this in a full inbox?

PREVIEW TEXT (40-90 characters, complements subject line)
  - Adds context the subject line couldn't fit
  - Do NOT repeat the subject line

OPENING (1-2 sentences)
  - Immediately relevant to the reader
  - No "I hope this finds you well" — get to the point
  - First-person or second-person voice

BODY (2-4 short paragraphs)
  - One idea per email (do NOT cram multiple CTAs)
  - Use line breaks generously
  - Bold key phrases for skimmers
  - Keep total word count under 300 for promotional, under 500 for newsletters

CTA BUTTON (one primary CTA)
  - Action-oriented text: "Get Your Free Trial" not "Click Here"
  - Visually distinct from body text
  - Repeat CTA as text link at the bottom (for email clients that strip buttons)

FOOTER
  - Unsubscribe link (REQUIRED — legal compliance)
  - Company address (REQUIRED — CAN-SPAM)
  - Social media links (optional)
```

**Email Writing Rules:**
- Write the subject line LAST (after you know what the email says)
- Create 3-5 subject line variations for A/B testing
- Preview text is NOT optional — if you don't write it, email clients pull random body text
- Segment when possible: different messages for different audience groups
- Respect frequency: check when the last email was sent before drafting another

**Subject Line Formulas:**
```
[Number] ways to [desired outcome]: "5 ways to reduce your invoicing time"
How [customer type] [achieved result]: "How freelancers are saving 10hrs/week"
[Outcome] without [objection]: "Grow your email list without annoying people"
The [adjective] guide to [topic]: "The honest guide to SEO in 2024"
[Question relevant to reader]: "Spending too much on marketing tools?"
```

### Ad Copy

**Search Ads (Google/Bing):**
```
HEADLINE 1 (30 chars): Primary keyword + benefit
HEADLINE 2 (30 chars): Differentiation or social proof
HEADLINE 3 (30 chars): CTA or urgency
DESCRIPTION 1 (90 chars): Expand on the value proposition
DESCRIPTION 2 (90 chars): Features, proof points, or secondary CTA
```

**Social Ads (Facebook/Instagram/LinkedIn):**
```
PRIMARY TEXT (125 chars visible, 500 max before truncation):
  - Hook: Problem or outcome in first line
  - Value: What do they get?
  - CTA: What should they do?

HEADLINE (25-40 chars): Clear, benefit-driven
DESCRIPTION (30 chars): Supporting info
CTA BUTTON: Choose from platform options (Learn More, Sign Up, Shop Now, etc.)
```

**Ad Copy Rules:**
- Always lead with the audience's problem, not your product
- Include specific numbers when possible ("Save 5 hours" not "Save time")
- Test emotional vs. logical appeals (different audiences respond differently)
- Never make claims you cannot substantiate
- Create 3-5 variations per ad for A/B testing

### Landing Pages

**Structure:**
```
HERO SECTION
  Headline: [Clear value proposition — what do they get?]
  Subheadline: [How — one sentence explaining the mechanism]
  CTA: [Primary action button]
  Social proof: [One stat, testimonial, or trust badge]

PROBLEM SECTION
  "You know the feeling..." (empathy with the reader's current pain)
  3 specific pain points (bullet points)

SOLUTION SECTION
  "Here's a better way" (introduce the product/service)
  3 key benefits (benefit-focused, not feature-focused)
  Each benefit: Icon + Headline + 1-sentence description

PROOF SECTION
  Testimonials (2-3 real quotes from real customers)
  Metrics (numbers that prove value)
  Logos (trusted brands that use the product)

CTA SECTION
  Restate the value proposition
  Address the #1 objection
  Clear CTA button
  Risk reducer ("Free trial", "Money-back guarantee", "No credit card required")

FAQ SECTION (3-5 questions)
  Address common objections as questions
  Keep answers short and confident
```

## Content Frameworks

Use these frameworks to structure persuasive content. Choose based on the content type and audience awareness level.

### AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
Best for: Ad copy, email campaigns, landing pages
```
A - Attention: Grab them with a bold statement, stat, or question
I - Interest: Explain why this matters to THEM specifically
D - Desire: Paint a picture of life with the solution (benefits, not features)
A - Action: Tell them exactly what to do next (one clear CTA)
```

### PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution)
Best for: Blog posts, email campaigns, sales pages
```
P - Problem: Identify the specific pain the reader has
A - Agitate: Make the pain vivid — what happens if they do nothing?
S - Solution: Present your solution as the answer (not a pitch, a resolution)
```

### BAB (Before, After, Bridge)
Best for: Case studies, testimonials, transformation stories
```
B - Before: Describe the customer's situation before the solution
A - After: Describe their situation after (specific, measurable improvement)
B - Bridge: Explain how the product/service was the bridge between the two
```

### 4U Formula (Urgent, Unique, Ultra-Specific, Useful)
Best for: Headlines, subject lines, social media hooks
```
Urgent: Create a reason to act now
Unique: Differentiate from every other headline
Ultra-Specific: Use numbers, names, details
Useful: Promise clear value to the reader
```

## Brand Voice Adaptation

### How to Apply Brand Voice

1. Read USER.md BRAND_VOICE section before writing anything
2. If the voice is not defined, default to "professional and approachable"
3. Apply the voice to every element: headlines, body copy, CTAs, even alt text

### Voice Translation Guide

| If brand voice is... | Headlines sound like | Body copy sounds like | CTAs sound like |
|---------------------|---------------------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Professional** | "Streamline Your Operations with [Product]" | Clear, precise, evidence-based. No slang. | "Request a Demo" |
| **Friendly** | "Make [Task] Way Easier (Finally)" | Conversational, uses "you" and "we". Warm. | "Try It Free" |
| **Casual** | "Okay, This Changes Everything" | Like texting a smart friend. Short sentences. | "Let's Go" |
| **Authoritative** | "The Definitive Guide to [Topic]" | Data-driven, expert tone, cites sources. | "Get the Report" |
| **Playful** | "Your [Problem]? Consider It Solved" | Fun, uses wordplay and humor. Still clear. | "Jump In" |

## A/B Testing

### When to Create Variations
- Email subject lines: ALWAYS create 3-5 variations
- Ad copy: ALWAYS create 3+ variations
- Landing page headlines: Create 2 variations when traffic allows
- Social media posts: Create 2 variations for important announcements
- Blog headlines: Create 2-3 options for customer to choose

### What to Test
- **Headlines**: Different hooks, frameworks, or word choices
- **CTAs**: Different action words, button text, placement
- **Length**: Short vs. long versions of the same content
- **Tone**: Emotional vs. logical appeal
- **Structure**: List vs. narrative, question vs. statement

### Variation Rules
- Change only ONE element per test (otherwise you do not know what worked)
- Label variations clearly: "Version A: Benefit-focused headline" / "Version B: Question headline"
- Present all variations to the customer with your recommendation and reasoning

## Review Checklist

Run this checklist on EVERY piece of content before presenting for approval.

```
CONTENT REVIEW CHECKLIST:

Accuracy:
[ ] All facts, stats, and claims are verifiable
[ ] No typos, grammar errors, or awkward phrasing
[ ] Links work (if included)
[ ] Dates, prices, and names are correct

Brand:
[ ] Matches brand voice from USER.md
[ ] Consistent with recent content (no contradictions)
[ ] Uses approved terminology (no banned words)
[ ] Tone is appropriate for the platform

Effectiveness:
[ ] Clear business goal (what should this content achieve?)
[ ] Strong hook in the first sentence/headline
[ ] Single, clear CTA
[ ] Written for the target audience (not too technical, not too simple)
[ ] Mobile-readable (short paragraphs, scannable structure)

Compliance:
[ ] No competitor disparagement
[ ] No unsubstantiated claims
[ ] Email includes unsubscribe link and physical address
[ ] Ad copy meets platform policies
[ ] No copyrighted content used without permission

SEO (if applicable):
[ ] Primary keyword in headline and first paragraph
[ ] Meta description written (150-160 chars)
[ ] Subheadings use related keywords naturally
[ ] Internal links included (if available)
```

## Content Repurposing

Maximize the value of every piece of content by repurposing across formats.

```
Blog Post (1500 words)
  → 5-10 social media posts (pull key insights)
  → 1 email newsletter (summarize with link to full post)
  → 1 LinkedIn article (adapt for professional audience)
  → 3-5 quote graphics (pull compelling sentences)
  → 1 thread (Twitter/X — break into numbered insights)
```

When repurposing:
- Adapt the content to each platform (do NOT just truncate)
- Spread posts over time (do NOT publish everything on the same day)
- Link back to the original when appropriate
- Track which format performs best for each content type
